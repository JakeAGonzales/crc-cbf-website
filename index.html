Safe Probabilistic Planning for Human-Robot Interaction using Conformal Risk Control

Jake Gonzales
University of Washington
Paper arXiv Code

Abstract

This paper presents a novel probabilistic safety framework for human-robot interaction that combines control barrier functions (CBFs) with conformal risk control (CRC) to provide formal safety guarantees without making distributional assumptions about human behavior. Safe deployment of autonomous robots in human environments remains challenging due to the inherent unpredictability in human actions, with traditional approaches either relying on restrictive distributional assumptions or defaulting to overly conservative worse-case bounds.

Our framework addresses these limitations by using CRC to quantify and control the prediction errors in CBF safety values, establishing formal guarantees on the probability of constraint satisfaction during interaction. We further introduce an online update scheme that dynamically adjusts the safety margins produced by CRC based on the current interaction context. Through extensive experiments in challenging human-robot navigation scenarios, we demonstrate that our approach reduces collision rates by an order of magnitude compared to standard CBFs while maintaining high success rates in goal-reaching tasks by avoiding unnecessary conservatism.
